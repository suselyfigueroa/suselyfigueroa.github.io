Academic Work

Research Topics

My mathematical research centres on integro-differential models, evolutionary dynamics, and their connection to real-world biological and medical phenomena.

Mathematics

Core areas of study

Six interrelated themes that form the backbone of my mathematical research.

01

Parabolic Integro-differential Models

Combining PDEs and integral equations to describe complex systems with diffusion and memory effects. Applications in physics, biology, and engineering where non-local interactions matter.

02

Selection–Mutation Models

Mathematical frameworks exploring genetic evolution by coupling natural selection and mutation processes. Essential in evolutionary biology for understanding phenotypic diversity over time.

03

Hamilton–Jacobi Equations

Fundamental equations in physics and optimisation that describe dynamic systems and paths of least resistance. Used to derive asymptotic results for structured population models.

04

Dynamics of Structured Populations

Analysis of how populations with diverse characteristics — age, phenotype, genotype — change over time. Crucial in ecology, epidemiology, and evolutionary medicine.

05

Stability & Hopf Bifurcation in Reaction-Diffusion Models

Investigation of equilibrium and oscillatory behaviour in spatially extended systems, vital for studying pattern formation and instabilities in biological tissues.

06

Applications in Evolutionary Biology & Medicine

Translating mathematical models into biological and clinical insights — from atherosclerosis dynamics to AI-assisted dermatology and epidemic modelling.

AI & Industry

AI & Data Science Work

Beyond pure mathematics, I apply rigorous analytical thinking to AI system design and validation.

A

AI Algorithm Validation

Designing and executing validation frameworks for AI/ML models with emphasis on robustness, fairness, and regulatory alignment in sensitive domains.

B

Computational Epidemiology

Wavelet-based approaches and Monte-Carlo Markov Chain algorithms applied to epidemic modelling, including Covid-19 dynamics.

C

AI in Dermatology

Computer vision and photo-analysis tools for skin condition assessment, dermoscope calibration, and quantification of skin lesions.
